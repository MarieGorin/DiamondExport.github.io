KIMBERLEY PROCESS

The Kimberley Process is a common initiative gathering governments, the diamonds industry and civil society entities
The Kimberly Process was born in May 2000, when the Southern African Countries which produced diamonds gathered in Kimberley, South Africa, to discuss the ways to put an end to the "War Diamonds" trade and to make sure the diamonds trade would not finance violent rebel movements' activities which aim at destabalizing the legitimate governments.
In November 2002, the negotiations between the governments, the international diamond industry's representatives and the civil society organizations led to the creation of the Kimberley Process Certification Scheme, which defines the conditions under which the production and trade of raw diamonds are controlled. It was implemented in 2003.
It became an efficient mechanism to fight against the war diamonds trade and a unique instrument in favor of peace and safety.
Tuas and Cecil is committed to using diamonds only from sources certified by the Kimberley Process.
